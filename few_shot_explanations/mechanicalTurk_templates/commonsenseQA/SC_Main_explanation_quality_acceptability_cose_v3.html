You will read multiple-choice questions about daily life. For each question, an answer has been selected and 9 explanations for the answer have been given.

Your task is to judge score each explanation in term of reasoning facts and reasoning structure in two-stage way


Each HIT contains 3 parts:

QuestionA question such as "If a lantern is not for sale, where is it likely to be?"

In the first stage, you will need to initially score each explanation in term of reasoning facts and reasoning structure via five-tier scoring schema as the following:

  • Reasoning Facts: Regarding quantity and quality of facts support reasoning, our reasoning facts scoring system prioritizes quality over quantity and is scored from 1 to 5 including Nonsensical Facts, Irrelevant or Erroneous Facts, Relevant but Insufficient Facts, Succinct yet Precise Facts and Sufficient and Precise Facts.
  • Reasoning structure: Regarding how well handles belief revisions in the face of conflicting information, our reasoning structure scoring system introduces a hierarchical system to manage complex structures and is scored from 1 to 5 including Chaotic Structure, Elementary Structure, Simple Structure, Comprehensive Structure, and Comprehensive with Exception Structure.

    • In the second stage, you will need to refine your score subsequent to initial scoring, annotators refine scores through pairwise comparisons of explanations with identical initial scores. Specifically when explanation E1 is considered superior to explanation E2 concerning reasoning structure or facts, the score of E1 is incremented by 0.5 in the pertinent aspect.
